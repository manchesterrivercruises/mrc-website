Finding a day out that genuinely works for the whole family is no small thing — it needs to be easy to get to, kind on the budget, and varied enough to keep children of different ages entertained. Salford Quays ticks every box. Compact, largely traffic-free and built around open water, it is one of the most family-friendly days out in Greater Manchester.

## Why it works for families

The great advantage of the Quays is that everything sits around a single basin, so there is very little trailing tired children between distant attractions. Wide, flat promenades are ideal for scooters and buggies, there is plenty of open grass to run off energy, and several of the best things to do cost nothing at all. Add in easy tram access and nearby parking, and the logistics — so often the undoing of a family outing — take care of themselves.

## A boat trip they will remember

For most children, the highlight is getting out on the water. A sightseeing cruise turns a day out into an adventure: the thrill of being on a boat, spotting bridges and other vessels, and watching the waterfront slide by. The trips are short enough to hold young attention spans and the open deck gives everyone a good view. It is also a lovely shared moment for the grown-ups — a chance to sit down together while the scenery does the entertaining.

## Free fun on land

Two nearby museums are both free and both surprisingly good with children. **IWM North** uses big, dramatic spaces and its immersive Big Picture Show to hold young imaginations, while **The Lowry** offers free galleries and often runs family activities and shows during school holidays. Between them, the promenades are made for feeding the ducks, watching the boats and simply pottering about by the water.

## Keeping everyone fed

Hunger is the enemy of any family day out, and happily the Quays are well stocked. The restaurants around the Lowry Outlet Mall are quick, reliable and used to young diners, while the waterfront spots near MediaCity offer pizza, burgers and ice cream with a view. On a fine day, the lawns beside the water make a brilliant picnic spot — bring a blanket and let the children spread out.

## A simple plan for the day

Aim to arrive mid-morning while everyone is fresh and start with the cruise, which sets the tone and gives you all your bearings. Break for an early lunch before anyone gets grumpy, then spend the afternoon at IWM North or the Lowry, with time on the promenade to burn off the last of the energy. Round things off with an ice cream by the water before heading home.

Easy to reach, gentle on the wallet and packed with variety, Salford Quays is that rare thing — a family day out that keeps children and parents equally happy, with the water at the centre of all the fun.
