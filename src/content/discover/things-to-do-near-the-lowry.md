The Lowry is the cultural anchor of Salford Quays — a landmark arts centre wrapped in shimmering steel and glass, sitting right at the water's edge. But part of its appeal is everything that surrounds it. Within a few minutes' walk you will find galleries, theatre, shopping, waterfront dining and open water, which makes it the perfect base for a day out.

## Start at The Lowry itself

Before you explore the area, it is worth spending time in the building that gives it its name. The Lowry houses the world's largest public collection of **L. S. Lowry** paintings and drawings, the artist whose matchstick figures captured industrial northern life. Alongside the galleries — which are free to visit — the centre runs a busy programme of theatre, musicals, dance and comedy across its stages. Checking what is on before you go can turn a daytime visit into a full evening out.

## Culture across the water

Directly opposite, reached by the footbridge, stands **IWM North**, the free Imperial War Museum housed in Daniel Libeskind's dramatic aluminium building. Between the two institutions you have two of the region's most significant cultural venues within sight of each other — an easy and inexpensive pairing that can fill the best part of a day.

## Shopping and dining

Right beside The Lowry is the **Outlet Mall**, handy for a spot of discount shopping and home to a cinema, so it is a useful fallback if the weather turns. When it comes to eating, the Quays are spoilt for choice. The waterfront terraces around MediaCityUK, a short walk or tram ride away, offer everything from relaxed pizza and burgers to smarter waterside dining. Closer to The Lowry you will find familiar chains that are quick and family-friendly between activities, plus cafés for a coffee and a pause.

## Get out on the water

For all the attractions on land, the Quays are defined by water — and the best way to appreciate that is to get out onto it. A sightseeing cruise from the nearby Millennium Footbridge takes you across the basin and along the historic Ship Canal, with commentary that explains how this former docklands became the cultural quarter it is today. It is a relaxing hour that gives real context to everything you can see from the promenades, and it slots neatly around a gallery visit or a show.

## A relaxed itinerary

There is no need to rush. Begin with the free galleries at The Lowry, cross the bridge to IWM North before lunch, then eat on the waterfront. Spend the afternoon on a cruise or browsing the Outlet Mall, and if there is a performance that catches your eye, stay on for the evening. Everything sits within a compact, walkable area around a single stretch of water, so you can pack in plenty without ever feeling stretched.

Whether you come for the art, the theatre, the shops or simply the setting, the area around The Lowry rewards a full and leisurely day — with the water always close at hand.
