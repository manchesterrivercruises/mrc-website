There is more than one way to reach Old Trafford on a matchday, but only one of them lets you skip the crowds, take in the waterfront and arrive by the water. Travelling to the football by boat has quietly become one of the most enjoyable ways for supporters to make the trip — part transport, part occasion.

## Why go by boat?

Anyone who has tried to reach Old Trafford on a big matchday knows the drill: packed trams, slow-moving traffic and a crush at the nearest stops both before and after the game. The ferry sidesteps all of it. Boarding at Salford Quays, you cruise along the historic Ship Canal to a landing close to the stadium, arriving relaxed rather than frazzled. There is space to sit, a proper view out over the water, and none of the stop-start shuffle of the road network.

## The matchday ferry experience

The journey is part of the fun. As you set off from the Quays, the modern waterfront of MediaCity gives way to the working history of the canal — wharves, bridges and the industrial bones of the port that made Manchester. On the way you will often find yourself among fellow supporters, and there is a genuine sense of build-up as the ground draws closer. A licensed bar onboard means you can start the day in good spirits, and the crew know the matchday routine inside out. For many regulars the ferry has become a ritual in its own right, as much a part of the day as the game itself.

## Timing and practical tips

Matchday services run to fit around kick-off, with sailings before the game and a return once the final whistle has gone. A few sensible pointers make for a smooth trip:

- **Arrive in good time.** Give yourself a comfortable margin before the scheduled departure — boarding is far calmer than a last-minute dash to the tram.
- **Book ahead.** Popular fixtures fill up, so securing your place in advance is wise.
- **Plan your return.** Decide before the game whether you are catching the return sailing, and factor in the walk back to the landing after full time.
- **Dress for the water.** Even in summer it can be breezy out on the canal, so bring a layer.

## More than just a way to get there

What sets the boat apart is that the travel becomes part of the outing rather than a chore to endure. You see a side of Manchester most visitors never do, you avoid the worst of the congestion, and you turn a routine journey into something memorable. Whether you are a season-ticket holder looking to shake up the usual routine or visiting the stadium for the first time, arriving by water gives the day a real sense of occasion.

Come the next big fixture, consider leaving the car and the tram behind. The Manchester Ship Canal delivered goods to and from this city for well over a century — these days it can deliver you to the match instead.
